# Ash's Objection Bible

THEY SAY: "It's too expensive for our small business."
ASH SAYS: Our Starter plan at ₹5,000/month is less than hiring a part-time content person. You get AI-powered consistency at a fraction of the cost — plus no training, no sick days.

THEY SAY: "We handle social media ourselves."
ASH SAYS: Consistency beats effort. Most owners post sporadically when free; we ensure daily, strategic content that builds real audience habits — while you focus on cooking.

THEY SAY: "Our customers are older, they don't use Instagram."
ASH SAYS: Even older demographics check Google Business and WhatsApp before visiting. We optimize for discovery and reminders — not just likes — so foot traffic grows.

THEY SAY: "We tried an agency before and got no results."
ASH SAYS: Most agencies sell activity, not outcomes. Opus builds automated systems that run whether you're watching — delivering posts, not promises.

THEY SAY: "I don't have time to give you content or approve posts."
ASH SAYS: We need one 15-minute monthly brief and batch approval. After setup, you can go zero-touch — we handle ideation, creation, scheduling.

THEY SAY: "Results take too long to show."
ASH SAYS: Algorithm favor consistency. Within 4 weeks of daily posting, you'll see increased profile visits and direct messages — early signals before foot traffic.

THEY SAY: "How are you different from Swiggy/Zomato marketing?"
ASH SAYS: They sell you to their platform; we build your own asset. Your Instagram becomes a permanent channel you own — no commissions, no dependency.

THEY SAY: "What if I don't like the posts you generate?"
ASH SAYS: You approve everything before it goes live. Our AI learns from your edits — so future posts need fewer changes, not more.

THEY SAY: "We don't have a big menu or events to post about."
ASH SAYS: Consistency itself is content. We turn daily specials, behind-the-scenes prep, and customer quotes into engaging posts — no event needed.

THEY SAY: "Social media feels fake for our traditional business."
ASH SAYS: We mirror your voice — whether it's Kannada proverbs, morning wish notes, or raw kitchen clips. Authenticity scales when systematized.

THEY SAY: "I don't believe in spending on marketing."
ASH SAYS: Treat it like a tiffin service for attention: small daily investment keeps you top-of-mind when hunger strikes. No marketing = invisible in hungry moments.

THEY SAY: "Our staff already handles posting; why pay extra?"
ASH SAYS: Your staff's time is worth more serving customers than chasing algorithms. We free them for higher-value work — like perfecting that dosa batter.
